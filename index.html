Section 1: Auditing (AUD)

AUD Question 1

A government internal audit function is presumed to be free from organizational independence impairments for reporting internally when the head of the organization

A. Is not accountable to those charged with governance.

B. Performs auditing procedures that are consistent with generally accepted accounting principles.

C. Is a line-manager of the unit under audit.

D. Is removed from political pressure to conduct audits objectively, without fear of political reprisal.

Correct Answer: D

Organizational independence for a government internal audit function is best achieved when its head is removed from political pressures. This allows the internal auditors to report objectively and without fear of reprisal, ensuring their findings are unbiased.

AUD Question 2

The controller of a small utility company has interviewed audit firms proposing to perform the annual audit of their employee benefit plan. According to the guidelines of the Department of Labor (DOL), the selected auditor must be

A. The firm that proposes the lowest fee for the work required.

B. Independent for purposes of examining financial information required to be filed annually with the DOL.

C. Included on the list of firms approved by the DOL.

D. Independent of the utility company and NOT relying on its services.

Correct Answer: B

The Department of Labor (DOL) requires that the auditor of an employee benefit plan be independent for the purpose of expressing an opinion on the financial statements filed with the DOL under ERISA. This independence is specifically outlined in the DOL's rules.

AUD Question 3

Which of the following employment relationships does not impair independence under SEC rules?

A. The Badger audit firm audits ABC Co., a publicly traded company. Tam is on Badger's audit team for the audit. His sister is a direct report of the CFO of ABC.

B. The Otter audit firm audits XYZ Co., a publicly traded company. Tim was on Otter's audit team for the audit last year, but just a few days after last year's audit was completed, Tim severed all ties with Otter and went to work as the controller at XYZ.

C. The Weasel audit firm audits LMN Co., a publicly traded company. LMN's audit cycle runs from January 1 to December 31. Tom was on Weasel's audit team for the 2016 audit. He severed all ties with Weasel on March 1, 2017. Tom went to work in a significant accounting position at LMN on January 2, 2019.

D. The Possum audit firm audits PQR Co., a publicly traded company. Max is lead partner on the PQR audit team. Max's sister Estrella is CFO of PQR. Max and his sister are estranged.

Correct Answer: C

SOX and SEC rules require a one-year "cooling-off" period before a former audit team member can take on a key financial reporting oversight role at an audit client. If a former auditor has served this required cooling-off period, their subsequent employment in such a role does not impair the firm's independence.

AUD Question 4

Which of the following are "covered persons" for purposes of the SEC's independence rules?

A. Sarah, a tax partner at her firm. She gave 15 hours of advice to her firm's audit team regarding the audit of ABC Co.

B. Tory, the chairman and senior partner at Sarah's firm.

C. Burt, the lead partner for the ABC Co. audit at Sarah's firm.

D. All of the options.

Correct Answer: D

Under SEC independence rules, "covered persons" are individuals whose financial relationships with an audit client are restricted. This includes members of the audit engagement team, partners who provide non-audit services to the audit client, as well as firm leadership who can influence the audit.

AUD Question 5

Sue's firm was hired to audit a Reno County project that used federal grant money. When the chief administrator heard about Sue's preliminary findings, he threatened that her firm would lose every audit contract with every unit of Reno County government if he was not pleased with Sue's audit report. This is an example of:

A. A potential personal impairment of independence.

B. A potential external impairment of independence.

C. A potential organizational impairment of independence.

D. None of the above.

Correct Answer: B

This scenario represents an external impairment to independence. External impairments occur when conditions or pressures from outside the audit organization prevent the auditor from carrying out the audit work and reporting the results impartially. A threat of losing future contracts due to an unfavorable audit report is a classic example of such external pressure.

AUD Question 6

In AICPA professional standards, the word "should" indicates a(n)

A. Interpretive suggestion that does not constitute a professional requirement.

B. Unconditional requirement with which the auditor is obligated to comply.

C. Presumptively mandatory requirement from which the CPA may depart in rare circumstances.

D. Recommendation that has no authoritative status.

Correct Answer: C

In AICPA professional standards, "should" indicates a presumptively mandatory requirement. The auditor must comply unless they can demonstrate that the alternative course of action taken was sufficient to achieve the objectives of the requirement. "Must" or "is required" indicates an unconditional requirement.

AUD Question 7

Interpretive publications include all of the following, except for

A. Appendices to Statements on Auditing Standards.

B. Articles in the AICPA's Journal of Accountancy.

C. Auditing guidance included in AICPA Audit and Accounting Guides.

D. Auditing interpretations of the Statements on Auditing Standards.

Correct Answer: A

Interpretive publications are recommendations on the application of Statements on Auditing Standards (SASs). SAS appendices are part of the SAS themselves, which are primary authoritative literature, not interpretive publications about the SAS.

AUD Question 8

An auditor's engagement letter most likely would include a statement that

A. Lists potential significant deficiencies discovered during the prior-year's audit.

B. Explains the analytical procedures that the auditor expects to apply.

C. Describes the auditor's responsibility to evaluate going-concern issues.

D. Limits the auditor's responsibility to detect errors and fraud.

Correct Answer: D

The engagement letter formalizes the agreement between the auditor and client. A crucial element is to clearly define the scope and limitations of the audit, including that an audit is designed to obtain reasonable assurance and may not detect all material misstatements, especially those resulting from fraud.

AUD Question 9

The scope and nature of an auditor's contractual obligation to a client ordinarily is set forth in the

A. Management letter.

B. Scope paragraph of the auditor's report.

C. Engagement letter.

D. Introductory paragraph of the auditor's report.

Correct Answer: C

The engagement letter is the formal written agreement between the auditor and the client. It outlines the terms and objectives of the engagement, the responsibilities of both management and the auditor, the scope of the audit, and the expected form and content of reports.

AUD Question 10

Which of the following procedures would an auditor most likely perform in the planning stage of an audit?

A. Make a preliminary judgment about materiality.

B. Confirm a sample of the entity's accounts payable with known creditors.

C. Obtain written representations from management that there are NO unrecorded transactions.

D. Communicate management's initial selection of accounting policies to the audit committee.

Correct Answer: A

Making a preliminary judgment about materiality is a critical step in the audit planning phase. This judgment guides the auditor in determining the nature, timing, and extent of audit procedures. Confirming accounts payable and obtaining management representations are typically performed during fieldwork and completion stages.

AUD Question 11

An auditor discovered that a client's accounts receivable turnover is substantially lower for the current year than for the prior year. This may indicate that

A. Fictitious credit sales have been recorded during the year.

B. Employees have stolen inventory just before the year end.

C. The client recently tightened its credit-granting policies.

D. An employee has been lapping receivables in both years.

Correct Answer: A

A lower AR turnover ratio indicates that accounts receivable are being collected more slowly or that the AR balance is unusually high relative to sales. Fictitious credit sales would inflate the accounts receivable balance without corresponding cash collections, thus lowering the AR turnover ratio.

AUD Question 12

The authority to accept incoming goods in receiving should be based on a(an)

A. Vendor's invoice.

B. Materials requisition.

C. Bill of lading.

D. Approved purchase order.

Correct Answer: D

In a well-controlled purchasing system, the receiving department uses an approved purchase order (often a blind copy without quantities) to verify that the goods received match what was authorized to be ordered. This provides the authority to accept the goods.

AUD Question 13

Which of the following control procedures would be most likely to assist in reducing control risk related to the existence or occurrence of manufacturing transactions?

A. Perpetual inventory records are independently compared with goods on hand.

B. Forms used for direct material requisitions are prenumbered and accounted for.

C. Finished goods are stored in locked limited-access warehouses.

D. Subsidiary ledgers are periodically reconciled with inventory control accounts.

Correct Answer: B

Prenumbered requisitions are a key control in the manufacturing process. They help ensure that all materials issued to production are properly authorized, recorded, and accounted for. This reduces the risk of unauthorized material usage, theft, or errors in costing production.

AUD Question 14

For which of the following matters should an auditor obtain written management representations?

A. Management's cost-benefit justifications for not correcting internal control weaknesses.

B. Management's knowledge of future plans that may affect the price of the entity's stock.

C. Management's compliance with contractual agreements that may affect the financial statements.

D. Management's acknowledgment of its responsibility for employees' violations of laws.

Correct Answer: C

The management representation letter includes assertions about management's responsibility for the financial statements, including their compliance with laws, regulations, and contractual agreements that could materially affect the financial statements.

AUD Question 15

An auditor concluded that no excessive costs for idle plant were charged to inventory. This conclusion is most likely related to the auditor's goal of obtaining evidence about which assertion regarding inventory at the end of the period?

A. Valuation and allocation.

B. Completeness.

C. Existence.

D. Rights and obligations.

Correct Answer: A

The assertion of valuation and allocation addresses whether assets are included in the financial statements at appropriate amounts. Including excessive idle plant costs (which should typically be expensed) in inventory would overstate the inventory's value, directly relating to the valuation assertion.

AUD Question 16

Which of the following conditions or events most likely would cause an auditor to have substantial doubt about an entity's ability to continue as a going concern?

A. Significant related party transactions are pervasive.

B. Usual trade credit from suppliers is denied.

C. Arrearages in preferred stock dividends are paid.

D. Restrictions on the disposal of principal assets are present.

Correct Answer: B

Denial of trade credit by suppliers is a strong indicator of an entity's inability to meet its obligations, signifying financial difficulties and raising substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern.

AUD Question 17

A government internal audit function is presumed to be free from organizational independence impairments for reporting internally when the head of the organization

A. Is not accountable to those charged with governance.

B. Performs auditing procedures that are consistent with generally accepted accounting principles.

C. Is a line-manager of the unit under audit.

D. Is removed from political pressure to conduct audits objectively, without fear of political reprisal.

Correct Answer: D

Under Government Auditing Standards (GAGAS, or Yellow Book), independence is crucial. For government internal audit functions, being free from political pressure is a critical aspect of maintaining objectivity and ensuring that audits are conducted without undue influence.

AUD Question 18

In planning a statistical sample for a test of controls, an auditor increased the expected population deviation rate from the prior year's rate because of the results of the prior year's tests of controls and the overall control environment. The auditor most likely would then increase the planned

A. Tolerable rate.

B. Allowance for sampling risk.

C. Risk of assessing control risk too low.

D. Sample size.

Correct Answer: D

In attribute sampling, if the auditor expects a higher deviation rate (meaning more errors or control failures), they will need a larger sample size to conclude with a given level of assurance that the actual deviation rate is within the tolerable rate.

AUD Question 19

An auditor is testing internal control procedures that are evidenced on an entity's vouchers by matching random numbers with voucher numbers. If a random number matches the number of a voided voucher, that voucher ordinarily should be replaced by another voucher in the random sample if the voucher

A. Constitutes a deviation.

B. Has been properly voided.

C. Cannot be located.

D. Represents an immaterial dollar amount.

Correct Answer: B

If a voucher is selected for testing and is found to be properly voided (e.g., a pre-numbered document that was spoiled and clearly marked as void according to policy), it means it never entered the population of valid transactions. In such a case, it would not represent a deviation, and the auditor would replace it with another randomly selected item.

AUD Question 20

Which of the following characteristics distinguishes electronic data interchange (EDI) from other forms of electronic commerce?

A. EDI transactions are formatted using standards that are uniform worldwide.

B. EDI transactions need not comply with generally accepted accounting principles.

C. EDI transactions are ordinarily processed without the Internet.

D. EDI transactions are usually recorded without security or privacy concerns.

Correct Answer: A

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is characterized by the use of standardized electronic formats (such as ANSI X12 or EDIFACT) to exchange business documents directly between computer systems of trading partners, enabling efficient and automated communication.

AUD Question 21

King, CPA, was engaged to audit the financial statements of Newton Company after its fiscal year had ended. King neither observed the inventory count nor confirmed the receivables by direct communication with debtors, but was satisfied concerning both after applying alternative procedures. King's auditor's report most likely contained a(n)

A. Qualified opinion

B. Disclaimer of opinion.

C. Unmodified opinion.

D. Unmodified opinion with an other-matter paragraph.

Correct Answer: C

If an auditor performs alternative procedures and these procedures provide sufficient appropriate audit evidence to fully satisfy the auditor regarding inventory and receivables, then there is no scope limitation. Consequently, an unmodified opinion is appropriate.

AUD Question 22

A limitation on the scope of an audit sufficient to preclude an unmodified opinion will usually result when management

A. Is unable to obtain audited financial statements supporting the entity's investment in a foreign subsidiary.

B. Refuses to disclose in the notes to the financial statements related party transactions authorized by the board of directors.

C. Does not sign an engagement letter specifying the responsibilities of both the entity and the auditor.

D. Fails to correct a significant deficiency communicated to the audit committee after the prior year's audit.

Correct Answer: A

A scope limitation arises when the auditor is unable to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence. If management cannot provide the financial statements or underlying records of a significant foreign subsidiary, the auditor's ability to gather evidence for a material portion of the consolidated financial statements is impaired.

AUD Question 23

A nonissuer uses a service organization whose services are part of the nonissuer's system of internal control. In the integrated audit, how does an auditor evaluate whether the service auditor's report on controls provides sufficient appropriate evidence to support an opinion on internal controls over financial reporting?

A. By inquiring of the service auditor's reputation only from the company's outside attorney.

B. By observing the service auditor to determine the level of knowledge about the entity under audit during the first week of the audit fieldwork.

C. By assessing the results of the tests of controls and the service auditor's opinion on the operating effectiveness of the controls.

D. By performing a background check of the service auditor.

Correct Answer: C

A user auditor evaluates a service auditor's report by reviewing the description of the service organization's controls, the tests of controls performed by the service auditor, the results of those tests, and the service auditor's opinion on the design and operating effectiveness of those controls.

AUD Question 24

An auditor most likely would be responsible for communicating significant deficiencies in the design of internal control structure

A. To the Securities and Exchange Commission when the client is a publicly held entity.

B. To specific legislative and regulatory bodies when reporting under Government Auditing Standards.

C. To a court-appointed creditors' committee when the client is operating under Chapter 11 of the Federal Bankruptcy Code.

D. To shareholders with significant influence (more than 20% equity ownership) when the significant deficiencies conditions are deemed to be material weaknesses.

Correct Answer: B

Under Government Auditing Standards (GAGAS or Yellow Book), auditors are required to communicate significant deficiencies and material weaknesses in internal control to those charged with governance and, when applicable, to legislative bodies or other oversight officials.

AUD Question 25

In reporting on an entity's internal control over financial reporting in an integrated audit of a nonissuer, an auditor should include a paragraph that describes the

A. Documentary evidence regarding the control environment factors.

B. Changes in internal control since the prior report.

C. Potential benefits from the auditor's suggested improvements.

D. Inherent limitations of internal control.

Correct Answer: D

A standard element of the auditor's report on internal control over financial reporting is a paragraph that discusses the inherent limitations of internal control. This explains that misstatements may occur and not be detected, and that projections of effectiveness to future periods are subject to the risk that controls may become inadequate.

Section 2: Business Environment & Concepts (BEC)

BEC Question 1

According to COSO, which of the following components addresses the need to respond in an organized manner to significant changes resulting from international exposure, acquisitions, or executive transitions?

A. Control activities

B. Risk assessment

C. Monitoring activities

D. Information and communication

Correct Answer: B

The Risk Assessment component of COSO focuses on identifying and analyzing relevant risks to the achievement of objectives. Significant changes like international expansion, acquisitions, and executive transitions introduce new risks or modify existing ones that must be assessed by management.

BEC Question 2

Which of the following is the best definition of a compensating control?

A. A control that accomplishes the same objective as another control.

B. A condition within an internal control system requiring attention.

C. The targets against which the effectiveness of internal control are evaluated.

D. Metrics that reflect critical success factors.

Correct Answer: A

A compensating control is a control put in place to mitigate the risk that would otherwise be left unaddressed by the absence or weakness of a primary control. It aims to achieve the same overall risk reduction objective, even if through different means.

BEC Question 3

This component of internal control concerns the policies and procedures that ensure that actions are taken to address the risks related to the achievement of management's objectives.

A. Control activities.

B. Control environment.

C. Monitoring.

D. Risk assessment.

Correct Answer: A

Control activities are the actions established through policies and procedures that help ensure that management's directives to mitigate risks to the achievement of objectives are carried out. These include authorizations, reconciliations, performance reviews, and segregation of duties.

BEC Question 4

This component of internal control concerns testing the system and its data.

A. Control activities.

B. Control environment.

C. Monitoring.

D. Risk assessment.

Correct Answer: C

Monitoring activities involve ongoing and/or separate evaluations to ascertain whether the components of internal control are present and functioning. Testing systems and data is a key aspect of monitoring to determine the effectiveness of controls.

BEC Question 5

Milo Corp. maintains daily backups of its accounting system in a fireproof vault in the file library. Weekly, monthly, and annual backups are stored in a secure, fireproof vault at an off-site location. Maintenance of the backup files is an example of

A. a detective control.

B. a feedback control.

C. a corrective control.

D. a preventive control.

Correct Answer: C

Backup file maintenance is a corrective control. It does not prevent an error or unauthorized event from occurring (preventive) nor does it detect it in real-time (detective). Instead, it allows for recovery and correction after a problem (like data loss or corruption) has occurred.

BEC Question 6

A government internal audit function is presumed to be free from organizational independence impairments for reporting internally when the head of the organization

A. Is not accountable to those charged with governance.

B. Performs auditing procedures that are consistent with generally accepted accounting principles.

C. Is a line-manager of the unit under audit.

D. Is removed from political pressure to conduct audits objectively, without fear of political reprisal.

Correct Answer: D

Independence is paramount for government auditors (as per GAGAS/Yellow Book). A critical factor for maintaining this independence is ensuring that the head of the audit organization is free from political pressure or influence that could compromise objectivity and integrity.

BEC Question 7

The currency or currencies of the Eurodollar market (Euromarket) include(s):

A. U.S. Dollar: Yes, EU Euro: Yes

B. U.S. Dollar: Yes, EU Euro: No

C. U.S. Dollar: No, EU Euro: Yes

D. U.S. Dollar: No, EU Euro: No

Correct Answer: B

The Eurodollar market refers specifically to U.S. dollars deposited in commercial banks outside the United States. The "Euro" prefix simply signifies "outside the U.S.," not the European currency (EU Euro).

BEC Question 8

International trade is measured by a country's

A. Imports: Yes, Exports: Yes

B. Imports: Yes, Exports: No

C. Imports: No, Exports: Yes

D. Imports: No, Exports: No

Correct Answer: A

International trade is primarily measured by the value of goods and services that a country buys from other countries (imports) and sells to other countries (exports). Both are essential components of trade statistics.

BEC Question 9

Which one of the following is least likely an advantage associated with a wholly owned foreign subsidiary?

A. Protection of proprietary information.

B. Ability to coordinate activities of the subsidiary with other activities.

C. Ability to maintain quality control.

D. Minimizes capital investment required.

Correct Answer: D

A wholly-owned foreign subsidiary requires a significant capital investment to establish or acquire, making it the least likely to minimize capital investment compared to other entry strategies like exporting, licensing, or franchising.

BEC Question 10

Which of the following statements regarding foreign licensing and foreign franchising, if any, is/are correct?
I. Licensors typically remain more involved with a licensee than a franchisor remains involved with a franchisee.
II. Franchising typically provides greater quality control than does simple licensing.

A. I only.

B. II only.

C. Both I and II.

D. Neither I nor II.

Correct Answer: B

Statement I is incorrect - licensors typically have less operational involvement than franchisors. Statement II is correct - franchising agreements are generally much more detailed and prescriptive, allowing franchisors to maintain strict control over quality, operations, and brand image.

BEC Question 11

In which of the following geographical areas has the greatest decline in share of worldwide output occurred over the past 40 years?

A. U.S.A

B. Europe.

C. Latin America.

D. Asia.

Correct Answer: B

Over the past 40 years, Europe (particularly Western Europe) has experienced a notable relative decline in its share of worldwide output as rapidly industrializing Asian economies have grown substantially. Asia, in contrast, has seen the greatest increase in its share.

BEC Question 12

The measurement of the benefit lost by using resources for one purpose and not another is

A. Sunk cost.

B. Opportunity cost.

C. Incremental cost.

D. Differential cost.

Correct Answer: B

Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative that must be forgone when making a choice. It represents the benefit lost from not using resources for the next best purpose.

BEC Question 13

A company forecast first quarter sales of 10,000 units, second quarter sales of 15,000 units, third quarter sales of 12,000 units and fourth quarter sales of 9,000 units at $2 per unit. Past experience has shown that 60% of the sales will be in cash and 40% will be on credit. All credit sales are collected in the following quarter. What amount of cash is forecasted to be collected in the second quarter?

A. $8,000

B. $18,000

C. $26,000

D. $30,000

Correct Answer: C

Q2 cash collections come from two sources: (1) Q2 cash sales: 15,000 units x $2 x 60% = $18,000, and (2) Q1 credit sales collected in Q2: 10,000 units x $2 x 40% = $8,000. Total Q2 cash collected = $18,000 + $8,000 = $26,000.

BEC Question 14

Which of the following U.S. GAAP levels of inputs for valuation purposes is/are based on observable inputs?

A. Level 1: Yes, Level 2: Yes, Level 3: Yes

B. Level 1: Yes, Level 2: Yes, Level 3: No

C. Level 1: Yes, Level 2: No, Level 3: No

D. Level 1: No, Level 2: No, Level 3: Yes

Correct Answer: B

Level 1 inputs are quoted prices in active markets for identical assets (observable). Level 2 inputs are observable inputs other than Level 1 quoted prices (e.g., quoted prices for similar assets). Level 3 inputs are unobservable inputs (e.g., management's own assumptions).

BEC Question 15

Which one of the following is not an element in the capital asset pricing model formula?

A. Risk-free rate of return.

B. Expected rate of return for the class of item being valued.

C. Prime interest rate.

D. A measure of volatility for the item being valued.

Correct Answer: C

The CAPM formula includes the risk-free rate, expected market return, and beta (volatility measure). The prime interest rate is a bank lending rate and is not a direct input to the CAPM formula, which uses a more theoretical risk-free rate (typically U.S. Treasury yields).

BEC Question 16

Which of the following roles is responsible for prioritizing systems development proposals?

A. IT steering committee.

B. Lead systems analyst.

C. Application programmers.

D. End users.

Correct Answer: A

The IT steering committee is typically a high-level governance body composed of key users and IT management. One of its primary responsibilities is to align IT projects with organizational strategy, evaluate project proposals, and prioritize systems development efforts.

BEC Question 17

Which of the following is responsible for identifying problems and proposing initial solutions?

A. IT Steering Committee.

B. Lead systems analyst.

C. Application programmers.

D. End users.

Correct Answer: B

A lead systems analyst (or business analyst) is responsible for working with users to understand existing processes, identify operational problems, gather requirements for new or improved systems, and propose initial solutions that address those needs.

BEC Question 18

In which of the following implementation approaches do the new and old systems run concurrently until it is clear that the new system is working properly?

A. Parallel.

B. Cold turkey.

C. Phased.

D. Pilot.

Correct Answer: A

In a parallel implementation strategy, both the old and new systems operate simultaneously for a period. This allows users to compare the output of both systems and verify the accuracy and functionality of the new system before the old system is completely phased out.

BEC Question 19

The requirements definition document is signed at this stage:

A. Planning and feasibility.

B. Analysis.

C. Design and development.

D. Implementation.

Correct Answer: B

The requirements definition document, which details what the new system must do, is finalized and signed off during the analysis phase of the SDLC. This formal approval ensures that all stakeholders agree on the scope and functionality before design and development begin.

BEC Question 20

Billy Bob's BarBQ has a small accounting staff and outsources payroll to a payroll service bureau. Which of the following is least likely to be an advantage of outsourcing payroll?

A. Improved accuracy

B. More accurate time recording by employees

C. Improved segregation of duties

D. Lower fraud risk

Correct Answer: B

Outsourcing payroll primarily relates to processing and disbursing payments. The accuracy of time recording (how employees track their hours) is an internal process that occurs before payroll processing and is not directly improved by outsourcing the payroll function itself.

BEC Question 21

Gram Co. develops computer programs to meet customers' special requirements. How should Gram categorize payments to employees who develop these programs?

A. Direct Costs: Yes, Value-adding costs: Yes

B. Direct Costs: Yes, Value-adding costs: No

C. Direct Costs: No, Value-adding costs: No

D. Direct Costs: No, Value-adding costs: Yes

Correct Answer: A

Wages paid to a software developer working specifically on a custom software project can be directly traced to that project (direct cost). Developing custom software creates a unique solution tailored to the client's specific needs, which increases the product's worth (value-adding activity).

BEC Question 22

A static budget contains which of the following amounts?

A. Actual costs for actual output.

B. Actual costs for budgeted output.

C. Budgeted costs for actual output.

D. Budgeted costs for budgeted output.

Correct Answer: D

A static (or master) budget is prepared for a single, planned level of activity. Therefore, it shows the budgeted costs at the budgeted (or planned) output level. It does not adjust for variations in actual activity.

BEC Question 23

Which of the following would be most impacted by the use of the percentage of sales forecasting method for budgeting purposes?

A. Accounts payable.

B. Mortgages payable.

C. Bonds payable.

D. Common stock.

Correct Answer: A

The percentage of sales method assumes that certain balance sheet accounts, particularly current assets and current liabilities, vary directly and proportionally with sales volume. Accounts payable typically increases as a company purchases more inventory to support higher sales.

BEC Question 24

The basic difference between a master budget and a flexible budget is that a master budget is

A. Based on one specific level of production, and a flexible budget can be prepared for any production level within a relevant range.

B. Only used before and during the budget period, and a flexible budget is only used after the budget period.

C. Based on a fixed standard, whereas a flexible budget allows management latitude in meeting goals.

D. For an entire production facility, whereas a flexible budget is applicable to single departments only.

Correct Answer: A

A master (static) budget is prepared for a single, planned level of activity. A flexible budget can be adjusted to any level of activity within the relevant range. This allows for a more meaningful comparison of actual results to what costs should have been for the actual output achieved.

BEC Question 25

On November 1, Year 1, a company purchased a new machine that it does not have to pay for until November 1, Year 3. The total payment on November 1, Year 3 will include both principal and interest. Assuming interest at a 10% rate, the cost of the machine would be the total payment multiplied by what time value of money concept?

A. Present value of annuity of 1.

B. Present value of 1.

C. Future amount of annuity of 1.

D. Future amount of 1.

Correct Answer: B

The cost of the machine is its present value - the equivalent cash price today. Since there is a single lump-sum payment in the future (not a series of payments), we use the present value of 1 factor to discount that future payment back to its current value.

Section 3: Financial Accounting & Reporting (FAR)

FAR Question 1

The following information pertains to Jet Corp. outstanding stock for Year 1: Common stock, $5 par value - Shares outstanding 1/1/01: 20,000; 2-for-1 stock split 4/1/Year 1: 20,000; Shares issued 7/1/Year 1: 10,000. Preferred stock, $10 par value, 5% cumulative - Shares outstanding 1/1/Year 1: 4,000. What are the number of shares Jet should use to calculate Year 1 earnings per share?

A. 40,000

B. 45,000

C. 50,000

D. 54,000

Correct Answer: B

Jan 1 - Mar 31: 20,000 shares x 2 (for split) = 40,000. Weighted: 40,000 x (3/12) = 10,000. Apr 1 - Jun 30: 40,000 shares. Weighted: 40,000 x (3/12) = 10,000. Jul 1 - Dec 31: 40,000 + 10,000 = 50,000 shares. Weighted: 50,000 x (6/12) = 25,000. Total: 10,000 + 10,000 + 25,000 = 45,000 shares.

FAR Question 2

A company had the following outstanding shares as of January 1, year 2: Preferred stock, $60 par, 4%, cumulative: 10,000 shares; Common stock, $3 par: 50,000 shares. On April 1, year 2, the company sold 8,000 shares of previously unissued common stock. No dividends were in arrears on January 1, year 2, and no dividends were declared or paid during year 2. Net income for year 2 totaled $236,000. What amount is basic earnings per share for the year ended December 31, year 2?

A. $3.66

B. $3.79

C. $4.07

D. $4.21

Correct Answer: A

A contract is voidable if one or both parties have the option to cancel or affirm it. REG Question 21

If an exempt organization is a corporation, the tax on unrelated business taxable income is

A. Computed at corporate income tax rates.

B. Computed at rates applicable to trusts.

C. Credited against the tax on recognized capital gains.

D. Abated.

Correct Answer: A

Unrelated Business Taxable Income (UBTI) of an exempt organization that is organized as a corporation is taxed at regular corporate income tax rates. If the organization is organized as a trust, it would be taxed at trust rates.

REG Question 22

On June 30, 2018, Berk retired from his partnership. At that time, his capital account was $50,000 and his share of the partnership's liabilities was $30,000. Berk's retirement payments consisted of being relieved of his share of the partnership liabilities and receipt of cash payments of $5,000 per month for 18 months, commencing July 1, 2018. Assuming Berk makes no election with regard to the recognition of gain from the retirement payments, he should report income therefrom of:

A. 2019: $13,333, 2020: $26,667

B. 2019: $20,000, 2020: $20,000

C. 2019: $40,000, 2020: $40,000

D. 2019: $0, 2020: $40,000

Correct Answer: A

Berk's outside basis: $50,000 + $30,000 = $80,000. Total proceeds: $30,000 (liability relief) + $90,000 (cash) = $120,000. Total gain: $120,000 - $80,000 = $40,000. Using installment method over 18 months of payments, gain is recognized proportionally as payments are received in 2019 and 2020.

REG Question 23

Danson and Ellerby are equal partners in DE Partnership, which is in the business of selling fine art. DE owns assets with a tax basis and fair market value of $240,000. In January of the current year, Finley contributes to the partnership some personal investment art with a fair market value of $120,000 (tax basis $80,000) to become a one third partner in the new DEF partnership. In October of the current year, DEF sells the art received from Finley for $141,000. What amount of gain from the sale of the artwork should be allocated to Finley?

A. $7,000

B. $20,333

C. $40,000

D. $47,000

Correct Answer: D

Under IRC Section 704(c), pre-contribution gain is allocated to the contributing partner. Pre-contribution gain: $120,000 - $80,000 = $40,000 (all to Finley). Total gain on sale: $141,000 - $80,000 = $61,000. Remaining gain after built-in: $61,000 - $40,000 = $21,000 (shared 1/3 each). Finley's share of remaining: $21,000 x 1/3 = $7,000. Total to Finley: $40,000 + $7,000 = $47,000.

REG Question 24

The standard deduction for a trust or an estate in the fiduciary income tax return is

A. $0

B. $650

C. $750

D. $800

Correct Answer: A

Trusts and estates do not receive a "standard deduction" like individuals. Instead, they are allowed an "exemption deduction" which is $600 for estates, $300 for simple trusts, and $100 for complex trusts. The standard deduction is $0.

REG Question 25

An executor of a decedent's estate that has only U.S. citizens as beneficiaries is required to file a fiduciary income tax return, if the estate's gross income for the year is at least

A. $400

B. $500

C. $600

D. $1,000

Correct Answer: C

An estate is required to file a fiduciary income tax return (Form 1041) if its gross income for the taxable year is $600 or more, or if it has a nonresident alien beneficiary.
